## 🎯 Project Overview
This project targets a critical business challenge for **Databel** (a telecom company): understanding why customers are leaving (**Churn**) and providing data-driven solutions to improve customer retention. 

By analyzing a comprehensive telecom dataset of **6,687 customers**, this analysis uncovers key behavioral patterns, demographic risk factors, and service flaws to transform raw data into operational revenue-saving strategies.

---

## 📈 Key Executive Metrics (KPIs)
* **Total Customer Base:** 6,687 customers
* **Total Churned Customers:** 1,796 customers
* **Overall Company Churn Rate:** **26.86%** *(Industry Benchmark Warning)*

---

## 🚀 The Data Analysis Pipeline
To ensure maximum reliability and business impact, the project followed a rigorous 4-step industry pipeline:

1. **Data Check & Validation:** Performed data cleaning, handled anomalies, and conducted structural sense-checks.
2. **Explore & Analysis:** Developed ad-hoc discovery dashboards to test hypotheses and uncover deep hidden patterns.
3. **Executive Dashboarding:** Filtered and migrated high-value charts into clean, grid-aligned, structured dashboards designed for stakeholder consumption.
4. **Communicate Insights:** Separated technical analysis into two tailored deliverables:
   * **Detailed Insights Document:** An exhaustive deep-dive analyzing each metric independently.
   * **Executive Stakeholder Report:** A high-level, 3-page presentation mapping out localized problems directly paired with corporate recommendations.

## 🖥️ Executive Dashboards Showcase

### 📂 Deliverable 1: Demographics & Location Analysis
*Focuses on WHO is churning and WHERE the crisis is concentrated.*

<p align="center">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ahmedyoussef542003/Churn-Analysis-Project-telecom-company/main/Images/p1.png" alt="Demographics Dashboard" width="800"/>
</p>

* **Problem 1 (Age Risk Factor):** Churn rate increases significantly as customers age, reaching a dangerous high of **33% to 44% for users over 60**.
  * *Recommendation:* Optimize and simplify digital touchpoints (UX) to accommodate senior users, and introduce tailored legacy retention offers or loyalty rewards.
* **Problem 2 & 3 (The Power of Group Plans):** Customers who are NOT part of a "Group Plan" have a drastically higher churn rate (**33%**) compared to those in a group (**7%**). Furthermore, group participation drops sharply for older demographics.
  * *Recommendation:* Launch targeted marketing incentives encouraging single and older customers to create or join "Family Group Plans" to secure long-term loyalty.
* **Problem 4 (Geographical Crisis):** **California (CA)** exhibits a critically high churn rate of **63%**, making it the most severe problem area compared to all other states.
  * *Recommendation:* Conduct an immediate localized competitive analysis in California to investigate rival pricing structures, network coverage, or customer support efficiency.

---

### 📂 Deliverable 2: Service & Contract Plan Analysis
*Focuses on WHAT product tiers and contract types are triggering customer exit.*

<p align="center">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ahmedyoussef542003/Churn-Analysis-Project-telecom-company/main/Images/p2.png" alt="Service Plan Dashboard" width="800"/>
</p>

* **Problem 1 (Unlimited Plan Friction):** Customers on Unlimited Data plans churn at **double the rate (32%)** compared to limited data users (16%), indicating the plan fails to build retention value.
  * *Recommendation:* Evaluate pricing models and check for hidden data-throttling issues. Enhance unlimited features to deliver premium perceived value.
* **Problem 2 (International Plan Inefficiency):** The highest churn spike (**over 70%**) originates from customers paying for an International Plan but **NOT actively making international calls**.
  * *Recommendation:* Proactively communicate with inactive international plan subscribers to downgrade or switch them to more suitable, cost-effective plans before they opt to leave completely.
* **Problem 3 & 4 (Contract & Tenure Vulnerability):** **Month-to-Month contracts** are highly volatile, driving a massive **46% churn rate** and accounting for 76% of total lost customers. New customers are the most vulnerable, with churn hitting **50% within the first 10 months**.
  * *Recommendation:* Incentivize short-term users to lock into 1 or 2-year agreements through multi-month discount incentives. Implement an intensive onboarding customer care program during the critical first 6 months.

---

### 📂 Deliverable 3: Operational Customer Support Analysis
*Focuses on WHY customers are leaving based on internal operations and competitors.*

<p align="center">
  <img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ahmedyoussef542003/Churn-Analysis-Project-telecom-company/main/Images/p3.png" alt="Customer Support Dashboard" width="800"/>
</p>

* **Problem 1 & 2 (Competitor Threats & Staff Behavior):** Competitor acquisition is the single biggest threat, driving over **800 customer losses** through better device offers and pricing models. This is heavily compounded by negative customer experiences linked to support staff attitudes.
  * *Recommendation:* Refurbish the company's device-upgrade catalog to counter rival hardware promotions, and invest in mandatory customer service empathy and technical training.
* **Problem 3 (The Support Call Threshold):** Customer support interactions reveal a strict breaking point: churn skyrockets to **88% after the 3rd support call** and hits a guaranteed **100% loss by the 4th/5th call**.
  * *Recommendation:* Deploy an automated early-warning flag on any account making their 2nd support call, automatically routing them to a specialized, senior retention squad empowered to grant immediate resolutions or loyalty discounts.
